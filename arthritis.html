What is Arthritis?

Arthritis is a common condition that causes pain, swelling, and stiffness in the joints. It can affect people of all ages, but it is more common in older adults. There are over 100 different types of arthritis, with osteoarthritis and rheumatoid arthritis being the most prevalent.

Causes

  • Wear and tear of joint cartilage (osteoarthritis)
  • Autoimmune disorders (rheumatoid arthritis)
  • Infections that affect the joints
  • Genetic predisposition
  • Injury or repetitive stress on joints

Symptoms

Common symptoms of arthritis include:

  • Joint pain and stiffness
  • Swelling and tenderness in the affected area
  • Reduced range of motion
  • Redness or warmth around the joint
  • Fatigue and general discomfort (especially in rheumatoid arthritis)

Exercises

  • Stretching: Improves joint flexibility.
  • Water Aerobics: Reduces joint strain.
  • Walking: Low-impact and joint-friendly.
  • Strength Training: Builds muscle to support joints.
  • Yoga: Enhances balance and mobility.

Treatment Options

Although there is no cure for arthritis, treatments can help manage symptoms and improve joint function:

  • Medications: Pain relievers, anti-inflammatory drugs, or disease-modifying antirheumatic drugs (DMARDs).
  • Physical Therapy: Exercises to improve strength, flexibility, and range of motion.
  • Assistive Devices: Using braces, shoe inserts, or walking aids to reduce strain on joints.
  • Surgical Options: Joint repair, replacement, or fusion in severe cases.
  • Lifestyle Changes: Maintaining a healthy weight, regular exercise, and a balanced diet.

Prevention

While arthritis may not always be preventable, you can reduce your risk by:

  • Maintaining a healthy weight to reduce stress on joints.
  • Engaging in regular low-impact exercises like swimming or walking.
  • Avoiding joint injuries by practicing proper techniques during physical activities.
  • Eating a diet rich in anti-inflammatory foods, such as fruits, vegetables, and omega-3 fatty acids.
