What is Depression?

Depression is a common and serious mental health condition that negatively affects how you feel, think, and act. It causes feelings of sadness and/or a loss of interest in activities you once enjoyed.

Causes

  • Biological Factors: Chemical imbalances in the brain.
  • Genetics: Family history of depression.
  • Environmental Factors: Trauma, abuse, or stressful life events.
  • Psychological Factors: Low self-esteem and pessimistic personality traits.

Symptoms

Common symptoms of depression include:

  • Persistent feelings of sadness or hopelessness
  • Loss of interest in activities
  • Changes in appetite and weight
  • Difficulty sleeping or oversleeping
  • Lack of energy or increased fatigue
  • Difficulty concentrating or making decisions
  • Thoughts of death or suicide

Exercises

  • Walking or Running: Releases endorphins to improve mood.
  • Yoga: Reduces stress and promotes relaxation.
  • Strength Training: Increases self-esteem and energy.
  • Dancing: A fun way to engage in physical movement.
  • Meditation & Breathing Exercises: Encourages mental clarity and calmness.

Treatment Options

Treatment for depression may include:

  • Psychotherapy: Talking with a mental health professional to address emotional issues.
  • Medications: Antidepressants prescribed by a healthcare provider.
  • Exercise: Regular physical activity can improve mood.
  • Support Groups: Connecting with others who have similar experiences.
  • Lifestyle Changes: Eating a healthy diet, maintaining a routine, and managing stress.

Management and Support

Managing depression involves ongoing support and self-care:

  • Practicing mindfulness or meditation
  • Building and maintaining a strong support network
  • Following your treatment plan consistently
  • Seeking help early when symptoms worsen
  • Focusing on small, achievable goals to build confidence
