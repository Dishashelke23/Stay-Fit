What is Diabetes?

Diabetes is a chronic condition that affects how your body turns food into energy. It is caused by either insufficient insulin production or the bodyâ€™s inability to use insulin effectively.

Causes

  • Type 1 Diabetes: An autoimmune condition where the body attacks insulin-producing cells.
  • Type 2 Diabetes: Often linked to lifestyle factors such as obesity and inactivity.
  • Gestational Diabetes: Develops during pregnancy due to hormonal changes.
  • Genetic and environmental factors may also play a role.

Symptoms

Common symptoms of diabetes include:

  • Increased thirst and frequent urination
  • Extreme hunger
  • Unexplained weight loss
  • Fatigue
  • Blurred vision
  • Slow-healing sores
  • Frequent infections

Exercises

  • Brisk Walking: Helps regulate blood sugar.
  • Strength Training: Enhances insulin sensitivity.
  • Swimming: A low-impact full-body workout.
  • Yoga: Reduces stress and promotes glucose control.
  • Cycling: Improves cardiovascular health.

Treatment Options

Treatment depends on the type of diabetes but generally involves:

  • Type 1 Diabetes: Insulin therapy is essential.
  • Type 2 Diabetes: Lifestyle changes, oral medications, and in some cases, insulin.
  • Gestational Diabetes: Monitoring blood sugar levels and adopting a healthy diet.
  • Regular monitoring of blood glucose levels.

Management and Support

Managing diabetes involves long-term lifestyle adjustments:

  • Eating a balanced diet rich in vegetables, whole grains, and lean proteins
  • Staying physically active
  • Maintaining a healthy weight
  • Attending regular check-ups with a healthcare provider
  • Joining diabetes support groups for emotional and practical guidance
