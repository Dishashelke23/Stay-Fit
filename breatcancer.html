What is Breast Cancer?

Breast cancer is a type of cancer that forms in the cells of the breasts. It is one of the most common cancers among women but can also affect men. Early detection and treatment can significantly improve outcomes.

Causes

  • Genetic mutations (e.g., BRCA1 and BRCA2 genes)
  • Family history of breast cancer
  • Hormonal imbalances
  • Age and gender (higher risk in older women)
  • Lifestyle factors such as diet and alcohol consumption

Symptoms

Common symptoms of breast cancer include:

  • Lump or thickening in the breast or underarm
  • Changes in the size, shape, or appearance of the breast
  • Skin changes, such as dimpling or redness
  • Nipple discharge other than breast milk
  • Pain or tenderness in the breast

Exercises

  • Stretching: Improves flexibility and reduces stiffness.
  • Walking: Gentle, low-impact exercise to rebuild endurance.
  • Strength Training: Helps regain muscle strength post-treatment.
  • Yoga: Enhances relaxation and body movement.
  • Swimming: Provides full-body low-impact movement.

Treatment Options

Treatment for breast cancer varies depending on the stage and type. Common treatments include:

  • Surgery: Removal of the tumor or breast (mastectomy or lumpectomy).
  • Chemotherapy: Use of drugs to destroy cancer cells.
  • Radiation Therapy: High-energy rays to target cancer cells.
  • Hormone Therapy: To block hormones that fuel certain types of breast cancer.
  • Targeted Therapy: Medications that specifically target cancer cells.

Prevention and Awareness

While not all breast cancer cases can be prevented, steps can be taken to reduce the risk:

  • Maintain a healthy diet and weight.
  • Exercise regularly and avoid excessive alcohol consumption.
  • Perform regular self-examinations and schedule routine mammograms.
  • Be aware of family medical history and consider genetic counseling if necessary.
