What is Chronic Fatigue Syndrome?

Chronic Fatigue Syndrome (CFS), also known as Myalgic Encephalomyelitis (ME), is a complex disorder characterized by extreme fatigue that doesnâ€™t improve with rest. It can significantly impact daily life and activities.

Causes

  • Viral infections (e.g., Epstein-Barr virus)
  • Immune system issues
  • Hormonal imbalances
  • Physical or emotional trauma
  • Unknown factors, as the exact cause is still being researched

Symptoms

Common symptoms of Chronic Fatigue Syndrome include:

  • Severe fatigue lasting more than six months
  • Unrefreshing sleep
  • Memory or concentration problems ("brain fog")
  • Muscle or joint pain
  • Sore throat or swollen lymph nodes
  • Headaches
  • Extreme exhaustion after physical or mental activities

Exercises

  • Gentle Yoga: Enhances flexibility and reduces fatigue.
  • Stretching: Helps relieve muscle stiffness.
  • Light Walking: Boosts circulation and energy levels.
  • Water Therapy: Reduces strain while maintaining movement.
  • Breathing Exercises: Promotes relaxation and oxygen intake.

Treatment Options

While there is no specific cure for CFS, treatments focus on symptom management and improving quality of life:

  • Medication: Pain relievers, sleep aids, or antidepressants as prescribed.
  • Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT): To address mental health challenges and coping strategies.
  • Graded Exercise Therapy (GET): A structured program to gradually increase physical activity.
  • Lifestyle Changes: Establishing a regular sleep schedule, stress management, and pacing activities.

Management and Support

Managing CFS involves adopting strategies that help reduce the impact of symptoms on daily life:

  • Keeping a symptom diary to identify triggers
  • Joining support groups for emotional and practical advice
  • Focusing on balanced nutrition and hydration
  • Communicating openly with healthcare providers
