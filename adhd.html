What is ADHD?

Attention Deficit Hyperactivity Disorder (ADHD) is a neurodevelopmental condition that affects both children and adults. It is characterized by persistent patterns of inattention, hyperactivity, and impulsivity that interfere with daily functioning or development.

Causes

  • Genetic factors and family history
  • Brain structure and function differences
  • Premature birth or low birth weight
  • Exposure to environmental toxins during pregnancy
  • Maternal substance use during pregnancy

Symptoms

Common symptoms of ADHD include:

  • Difficulty sustaining attention
  • Frequent forgetfulness and losing items
  • Excessive fidgeting or restlessness
  • Interrupting or intruding on others during conversations
  • Difficulty organizing tasks and activities

Exercises

  • Cardio Workouts: Running, cycling, or swimming to enhance focus.
  • Yoga and Meditation: Helps improve mindfulness and concentration.
  • Strength Training: Provides structure and goal-setting for focus improvement.
  • Martial Arts: Enhances discipline and motor skills.
  • Dancing: Encourages coordination and cognitive engagement.

Treatment Options

ADHD can be effectively managed with the following:

  • Medications: Stimulants like methylphenidate and non-stimulants to improve focus and control impulses.
  • Behavioral Therapy: Helps develop coping strategies and organizational skills.

  • Educational Support: Accommodations in schools to help children thrive academically.
  • Lifestyle Changes: Maintaining routines, exercise, and a healthy diet.

Support Strategies

To help individuals with ADHD succeed:

  • Provide consistent structure and routines.
  • Break tasks into smaller, manageable steps.
  • Use reminders and organizational tools like planners.
  • Encourage regular physical activity and stress management techniques.
